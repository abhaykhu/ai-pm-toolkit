# Enablement Asset Types Reference

## Sales Pitch Deck (Outline + Speaker Notes)

**Format**: Slide-by-slide outline with speaker notes for each slide.

### Template
```markdown
# [Product/Feature] Pitch Deck

## Slide 1: Title
**Visual:** [Product name, tagline, logo]
**Speaker notes:** [Opening hook, introduce yourself]

## Slide 2: The Problem
**Visual:** [Problem statement, pain point data]
**Speaker notes:** [Paint the picture — "Have you ever..."]

## Slide 3: Current Reality
**Visual:** [How prospects handle this today]
**Speaker notes:** [Acknowledge workarounds, quantify cost]

## Slide 4: The Solution
**Visual:** [Product overview, key value prop]
**Speaker notes:** [Bridge from problem to solution]

## Slide 5-7: Key Capabilities
**Visual:** [Feature + benefit for each]
**Speaker notes:** [Concrete example, customer quote if available]

## Slide 8: Social Proof
**Visual:** [Logos, testimonials, metrics]
**Speaker notes:** [Reference specific customer success]

## Slide 9: Differentiation
**Visual:** [Why us vs. alternatives]
**Speaker notes:** [Focus on unique advantages]

## Slide 10: Call to Action
**Visual:** [Next steps, contact, pricing]
**Speaker notes:** [Clear ask, create urgency]
```

## Competitive Battle Card

**Format**: One-page reference for sales conversations.

### Template
```markdown
# Battle Card: [Your Product] vs. [Competitor]

## Quick Summary
[One sentence on competitor and how we differ]

## When You'll Encounter Them
[Deal types, segments, regions]

## Their Strengths (Be Honest)
- [strength — acknowledge it]

## Our Advantages
- [advantage with proof point]

## Common Objections & Responses
| They Say | You Say |
|----------|---------|

## Landmines to Set
[Questions to ask prospects that highlight competitor weaknesses]

## Trap Questions (& How to Handle)
[Questions competitors coach prospects to ask you]

## Win/Loss Patterns
[When we tend to win vs. lose]
```

## Objection Handling Guide

### Template
```markdown
# Objection Handling Guide: [Topic]

## Objection: "[exact words]"
**Why they say it:** [underlying concern]
**Response framework:** [acknowledge → reframe → evidence → redirect]
**Example response:** "[scripted response]"
**Follow-up question:** "[question to continue conversation]"
```

```markdown
### Scene 2: [Core Value]
[The "wow" moment]

### Scene 3: [Differentiation]
[What competitors can't do]

## Q&A Prep
[Anticipated questions and answers]

## Closing (2 min)
[Summary, next steps, call to action]
```
